About Me

My name is Alexander Frummet and I am PhD Student and lecturer at the Chair for Information Science at the University of Regensburg which is located right in the heart of Bavaria, Germany. Prior to pursuing a doctoral degree, I studied General and Comparative Linguistics and Information Science (both Bachelor degrees) and Media Informatics (Master's degree) at the University of Regensburg.

For more information have a look at my CV.

Research Interests

Having a background in both linguistics and computer science related fields, my research interests fall in the intersection of Natural Language Processing and Information Retrieval - conversational search. In my PhD project, which is supervised by Dr David Elsweiler, I aim to understand which user information needs arise when people are cooking with a conversational assistant, how these needs can be automatically detected by systems and which algorithms are most suitable for satisfying these information needs within a running conversation.
